The Public Theatre

The Public Theater is an arts organization based in New York City that aims to develop and produce plays from up-and-coming playwrights and performers.

"The public is a theater of, by and for all people"

Inspirations

Wim Crouwel

During the ideation we drew our inspiration from Wim Crouwel, Dutch typographer and graphic designer, famous for the use of grid-based layouts and typography that is rooted in the International Typographic Style. We specifically examined his iconic use of grid to rearrange type into more intriguing forms.

Ellen Lupton

"Graphic Design: The New Principles" by Ellen Lupton was also used to derive insights to drive the project. Our main takeaway was that by looking at the constraints of a system from a new and fresh perspective, unpredictable results can be produced.

Synthesis of Both

Looking at Wim Crouwel's and Ellen Lupton's principles inspired us to consider how we could utilize the constraints of the grid to experiment with type and imagery as a way to create unpredictable results that brings attention to the message.
